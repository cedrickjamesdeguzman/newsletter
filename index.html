Environmental Caravan

ENVIRONMENTAL CARAVAN FOR RESPONSIBLE COMMUNITY GARBAGE SEGREGATION

The project is aimed at promoting responsible garbage segregation through recycling. During the project implementation, residents surrendered their bottles, plastics, boxes, papers, bakal, yero and lata, in exchange for sustainable goods. Busted bulbs, lamps and fluorescents were traded to more efficient and environment friendly LED bulbs. A total of 440 kilograms of mixed recyclables, 1,925 pieces of assorted bottles and 195 pieces of busted lamps/bulbs were collected by the CENRO employees at the end of the recycling caravan.

Good Practices of LGUs

ICYMI: SAN FERNANDO RECEIVES AWARD FOR THE IMPLEMENTATION OF JUVENILE JUSTICE AND WELFARE ACT

586 JOBSEEKERS

2nd wave of the People’s Job Fair

The event was participated by 33 local and 15 overseas employers. Aside from the employers, two national government agencies also joined the event. The Department of Foreign Affairs (DFA) catered passport applications from 11 individuals and the Philippine Postal Corporation (PhilPost) provided Postal ID application forms to 34 individuals. Mikka Francesca Hipol, spoke in behalf of Manong Dong, the Chief–of-Staff of the City Mayor who said that given the right opportunities, each of us is capable of taking part in building a city we can all be proud of. The fair was organized by the City Government of San Fernando, La Union Public Employment Service Office (PESO) in partnership with PHILHEALTH, PAG-IBIG FUND, SSS, DFA, PGLU, DOLE, TESDA, OWWA and POEA.
Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs)

The vision of having socially and economically empowered citizens

The Local Economic and Business Development Office led by Rizalyn D. Medrano, facilitated a Focus Group Discussion held at City’s Trade Center on December 12-13, 2018. MSME stakeholders, and the MSME Development Council were called to raise problems and address challenges regarding several business aspects that needs to be focused on. Similarly, discussions for proposed solutions were also noted and covered. The FGD aims to help address the problems of MSME and recognize the MSME sector’s contributions for the city’s economic growth and development.
